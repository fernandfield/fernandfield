Zero-ish Waste Living

Explore living a low-zero-ish waste lifestyle. Our participants will learn all simple eco friendly swaps they can make at home, at work and when on the go. They will also learn what goes where in the recycling bin equiping them with the knowledge on how to reduce, repair and reuse.

Think Good Food

669,000 tonnes of food is wasted annually in Singapore, equivalent to about two bowls of rice per person daily. Food waste is a significant contributor to climate change. In our sessions, we will share practical and easy tips to help you reduce your food waste and make a positive impact on the environment.

Contact Us

The Textile Project

In this 2-hour workshop, participants will explore the environmental impact of the fashion industry and learn how to make their wardrobe more sustainable. Through hands-on activities like sewing a button, patching holes, and repurposing old clothes—think turning worn-out jeans into stylish sling bags! With tips on fabric care and upcycling, this session equips you to reduce waste, extend the life of your clothes, and contribute to a greener planet.

Contact Us

Learn Biodiversity things

we will dive into the wonders of biodiversity in this interactive workshop where participants will explore the diverse ecosystems that make our planet thrive. Learn about local wildlife, the importance of preserving natural habitats, and how small actions can contribute to protecting our environment. Through fun activities and discussions, you’ll uncover the vital role biodiversity plays in maintaining a balanced and healthy world. Join us and reconnect with the beauty and learn the intricacies of nature!

Contact Us

Love Your Body

We’ll help participants embrace sustainable beauty care by replacing not-so-sustainble products with eco-friendly alternatives like shampoo bars, reusable razors, and sustainable period care products. Learn how to streamline your self-care routine, reduce bathroom clutter, and create a relaxing, low-waste sanctuary. Plus, get hands-on with the option to make your own natural soy wax candle, face mask, body scrub for that extra glow—and many more!

Our Testimonials

On behalf of Changi Simei Community Club, I would like to thank John and his team at ReThink Good (RTG) for joining us at Eco Green Bazaar 2024 held in conjunction with Changkat Changi Secondary School. RTG held a booth on plastic upcycling and demonstrated the process of turning grinding and melting unwanted plastic waste (such as bottle caps) into useful household items such as carabiners and combs.

The booth had managed to gather much interest and positive feedback from our residents, students and teachers present at the event. Many praised the booth’s hands-on demonstration using machines which can be easily operated by our residents, thereby enhancing the booth’s engagement value and outreach to the public. We are pleased and satisfied at RTG’s commitment to educating the public on sustainability practices and we hope to collaborate more with the team for future events.

Felix, Committee member and event organiser

The workshop has helped the youth to better understand the environmental impact of not recycling plastics in Singapore and understand the movement towards a "Circular Economy". They enjoyed and were really happy to be able to turn their recycled plastic bottle caps into something useful, like the carabiner and comb. Thank you ReThink Good for this hands-on experience!

Joycelyn, Youth Guidance

Our facilitator Jeryl Yep was engaging and knowledgeable and, his and his team’s dedication to the cause was evident. He shared with us how waste could be turned into functional items, educated us on the different types of plastics and the different resin identification codes as well as the long-lasting impact recycling would make on the environment.

Pamela Wong, Head of Community Partnerships, Singapore Cancer Society
